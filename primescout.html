An Umanah Systems Group Program

PrimeScout

Governed autonomous patrol and inspection for indoor and outdoor environments.

PrimeScout combines autonomous mobile systems, low-light sensing, terrain and route intelligence, anomaly-aware behavior, and governed execution through Reydious.

Complete patrol routes. Verify checkpoints. Inspect assigned areas. Produce auditable mission records.

Request Pilot Discussion System Approach
Challenge

The Problem

In many operational environments, fixed cameras leave blind spots and human patrols are expensive, inconsistent, or difficult to sustain across every hour of the day.

That creates gaps in route coverage, checkpoint verification, perimeter awareness, and off-hours inspection.

PrimeScout is designed to close that gap with governed autonomous patrol and inspection.

Solution

System Approach

PrimeScout is an autonomous site operations system built around five integrated layers.

01

Mobile Patrol Platform

A dockable autonomous system designed to move through structured indoor and outdoor environments.

  • Supports scheduled patrol and inspection routes
  • Built for repeatable route execution across defined operating areas
  • Designed for persistent site coverage with return-to-dock behavior
02

Low-Light Perception

A sensing layer designed for conditions where conventional vision systems become less reliable.

  • Supports navigation and inspection in low light, glare, and mixed-visibility conditions
  • Improves hazard detection and route confidence
  • Enables more reliable off-hours operation
03

Route and Terrain Intelligence

A route-assurance layer for constrained, structured environments.

  • Maintains route logic across indoor and outdoor zones
  • Supports checkpoint sequencing and traversal planning
  • Enables safer reroute, slowdown, stop, or return behavior
04

Anomaly Interpretation

A decision layer for distinguishing transient issues from operationally relevant anomalies.

  • Flags off-normal conditions during patrol and inspection
  • Supports continue, slow, stop, reroute, dock, or escalate decisions
  • Connects observations to mission context instead of treating every alert the same way
05

Governed Execution

Powered by Reydious. Governs how the machine behaves before and during mission execution.

  • Enforces route, zone, and behavior constraints
  • Applies authority rules and mission conditions
  • Records what the machine did, why it did it, and when it stopped or escalated
06

Mission Evidence

A record layer for reviewable patrol and inspection evidence.

  • Captures checkpoint proof and route completion records
  • Organizes logs for review, exception handling, and audit
  • Shows what was completed, missed, or escalated

PrimeScout is built for governed patrol and inspection with auditable execution.

Deployment

Deployment Environments

PrimeScout is designed for structured environments where route completion, checkpoint verification, and accountable machine behavior matter.

Deployment sequencing will depend on pilot fit, site constraints, and mission requirements.

Target environments include:

  • warehouses and logistics facilities
  • construction sites
  • industrial campuses
  • equipment and storage yards
  • utilities and energy sites
  • commercial and business campuses
  • restricted and defense-adjacent environments

Differentiation

The Wedge

PrimeScout delivers governed autonomous patrol and inspection, not just detection or monitoring.

That distinction is the wedge.

Most systems focus on watching, alerting, or deterrence. PrimeScout is built to complete bounded operational work under defined rules.

  • Routes are assigned.
  • Checkpoints are verified.
  • Patrol behavior is constrained.
  • Anomalies are interpreted in context.
  • Escalation and return behavior can be governed by policy.
  • Each mission leaves behind auditable evidence of completion, exception, interruption, or override.
Validation

Pilot Invitation

PrimeScout is currently seeking pilot environments where governed autonomous patrol and inspection can be evaluated under real operating conditions.

Pilot objectives include:

  • route completion reliability
  • checkpoint verification accuracy
  • low-light patrol performance
  • indoor and outdoor traversal continuity
  • policy enforcement during off-normal conditions
  • docking, recovery, and return behavior
  • audit trail completeness and operator reviewability
Measurement

Pilot Evaluation Metrics

The pilot is intended to measure:

  • route completion rate
  • checkpoint verification consistency
  • patrol uptime across assigned schedules
  • low-light navigation reliability
  • false positive and false escalation rates
  • mission log completeness
  • operator usability and workflow fit
  • time from anomaly detection to structured review

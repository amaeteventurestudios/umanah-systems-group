BioGrid Networks

Distributed microscopy-based biological sensing for remote industrial and defense environments.

BioGrid combines portable hardware built under the Crucible framework with a governed operating system powered by Reydious.
Reduce detection-to-decision time. No lab. No delay. No uncertainty.

Request Pilot Discussion See How It Works
Mission

The Problem

In remote industrial and operational environments, biological issues are often detected too late.

Samples are transported offsite. Results are delayed. Operations continue under uncertainty.

That delay increases operational exposure. BioGrid is designed to reduce detection-to-decision time.

Solution

System Approach

BioGrid is distributed biological sensing infrastructure built around three integrated components.

01

Biological Edge Node

A portable microscopy-based Edge Node built under the Crucible hardware framework.

  • Operated by trained personnel
  • Designed for dust, humidity, vibration, and infrastructure-limited environments
02

BioGrid Operating System

Powered by Reydious. Structures every biological observation.

  • Records time, location, operator, and sample context
  • Creates auditable records and escalation pathways
03

Data Fabric

Structured storage and access across deployment sites.

  • Secure storage with role-based access
  • Cross-site comparison
  • On-prem or secure cloud deployment options

Scope clarification:

  • BioGrid does not perform DNA sequencing
  • It does not perform PCR testing
  • It does not conduct molecular diagnostics

Validation

Pilot Invitation

BioGrid Networks is currently seeking 3–5 pilot sites in remote or operationally constrained environments.

Pilot objectives include:

  • Field slide preparation reliability
  • Clear image capture under operational conditions
  • Structured OS logging and audit trail validation
  • Multi-site data aggregation and comparison
  • Operational friction and workflow integration
Measurement

Pilot Evaluation Metrics

The pilot is intended to measure:

  • Detection accuracy under field conditions
  • Image capture reliability across environments
  • OS logging completeness and audit trail integrity
  • Time from sample collection to structured record
  • Operator usability in infrastructure-limited environments
  • Integration into existing compliance and reporting workflows
